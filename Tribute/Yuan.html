Yuan Longping

Father of hybrid rice

Here's a timeline of Yuan Longping's life:

  • 1930 - Born in Beijing, China
  • 1953 - Graduated from Southwest Agricultural College
  • 1960 - He had the idea of hybridizing rice to increase its yield after reading of similar research that was underway successfully in maize and sorghum
  • 1970 - Beside a railway line in Hainan, he and his team found a particularly important wild variety.[14] Using this one within a breeding programme resulted in varieties with yields improved by 20 - 30% in the late 1970s
  • 1999 - The "Super Rice" Yuan worked on improving showed a 30 percent higher yield, compared to common rice, with a record yield of 17,055 kilograms per hectare being registered in Yongsheng County in Yunnan Province
  • 2001 - Awarded State Preeminent Science and Technology Award
  • 2004 - Awarded Wolf Prize in Agriculture and World Food Prize
  • 2012 - Awarded Confucius Peace Prize
  • 2014 - Yuan said in an interview that genetically modified food would be the future direction of food and that he had been working on genetic modification of rice
  • 2019 - Awarded Order of the Republic
  • 2021 - Yuan Longping collapsed at his hybrid rice research base in Sanya
